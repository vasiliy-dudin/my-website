# Seniority Signals and Market Context

Load this file when assessing whether work is positioned at the right seniority level, or when providing strategic context about how portfolios are evaluated in 2025–2026.

---

## Seniority signals

Hiring managers calibrate seniority from portfolios. Senior-level work shows:

- **Ambiguity handled:** unclear brief, no precedent, competing stakeholder needs.
- **Cross-functional influence:** changed how a team worked, not just what it built.
- **Systems thinking:** decisions that account for edge cases, future states, and scale.
- **Trade-offs named:** what was left on the table and why, not just what shipped.
- **Working without constant direction:** proposing solutions rather than executing requests.
- **Understanding of engineering and business constraints** — not just user needs.

Mid-level signals to avoid over-emphasising at senior level:
- Execution quality on defined tasks
- Following an established process
- Individual screen-level decisions
- Tool proficiency listed without context

---

## Market context (2025–2026)

What product companies prioritise when reviewing portfolios, in order:

1. **Product thinking and problem framing** — understanding the problem before the solution.
2. **Demonstrated impact** — outcomes, not just deliverables. Metrics where available; qualitative change where not.
3. **Clear articulation of role** — the distinction between "I designed X" and "the team shipped X" matters.
4. **Cross-functional fluency** — evidence of working effectively with PMs, engineers, researchers.
5. **Visual craft** — assumed at senior level; weak execution is disqualifying.
6. **Systems thinking** — design systems experience, tokens, governance, cross-team adoption.

**Common red flags hiring managers cite:**
- No outcomes — only outputs described
- Generic language that could belong to any designer
- Over-claiming: "Led the redesign of…" when one of five designers
- Process-heavy case studies that never reach outcomes
- Polished UI with no explanation of decisions
- No evidence of research or validation
